In physics, the "Principle of Least Action" is one of the most elegant concepts in the universe. It states that nature is lazy. Light doesn't check every possible path before traveling; it simply takes the path that minimizes "action." It is efficient. It is optimized.

My path to understanding physics has been anything but.

### The "Garage Scientist" Approach
I am not a PhD candidate. I am not currently enrolled in a university. I am a self-taught tinkerer with a background in sales and automation, a basement full of electronics, and a dangerous amount of curiosity.

This blog, *The Least Action Principle*, is my digital lab notebook. It is an attempt to optimize a very chaotic journey: taking a brain that hasn't done serious math in 12 years and training it to understand the fundamental laws of the universe.

### The Trajectory
The goal is simple, even if the execution is not: **To derive the motion of the universe from scratch.**

We are starting at **Level 0**:
* **Math:** Rusty High School Algebra.
* **Physics:** Basic Newtonian concepts (F=ma).
* **Tools:** Python, Jupyter Notebooks, and a PixelBook.

We are aiming for **Level 100**:
* **The Text:** Landau & Lifshitz, *Course of Theoretical Physics, Vol 1: Mechanics*. (Known as the "Dark Souls" of physics textbooks).
* **The Math:** Calculus of Variations, Lagrangian Mechanics, and Differential Equations.
* **The Application:** Building high-fidelity computer simulations of orbital mechanics and particle systems.
